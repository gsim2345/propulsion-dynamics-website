How the CASPER service works

While your ship is at sea the crew on the vessel records performance data at periodic intervals and forwards the data to our office.

Our naval architects then analyze the data and generate a report that provides precise calculations of speed, fuel consumption and resistance in relation to the ship sea trial data (physical model).

For each set of data, we calculate:

  • the propeller thrust
  • speed through water
  • ship resistance, as new, based on calculated speed
  • ship resistance, actual, based on calculated speed
  • added resistance as the difference between #4 and #5 above

All data is fully corrected for wind, waves, sea current, fuel oil quality, water temperature and other variables.

The CASPER Service can be initiated on any ship at any time and does not require additional software or equipment.

COLLECTING AND ANALYZING DATA

Collecting vessel performance data is done by the crew or (or monitoring systems) while sailing, using our proprietary instructions and then forwarded to our technical office by email or web interface.

Our performance data format is a highly evolved procedure that improves the crew's ability to record accurate parameters.

This data is analyzed onshore by our Naval Architects, using advanced computerized methods in conjunction with the ship particulars and sea trial data. Reports are submitted to the ship's technical staff at regular intervals.

We are also able to acquire data from weather routing or other performance monitoring systems for use in our services. In this way, our services adapt to either auto logged data or crew data collection protocols.

CASPER REPORTS

CASPER reports show accurate figures for speed and fuel penalty due to hull and propeller fouling in relation to the clean smooth hull and physical model from sea trials.

It also shows what benefits are attainable by propeller polishing and hull cleaning in terms of reduced fuel consumption or higher service speeds.

In addition, we offer Sea Trial Analysis, Dry Dock Analysis, Trim Optimization, and validation of Charter Party description (speed, draft, consumption). Our customers save money by acting on the information in our reports, not by merely knowing trends.

Reports are submitted to the ship's technical staff at regular intervals.

HOW TO USE THE REPORTS

The real value is the innovative way in which our clients utilize the information. Here are some ways clients use our reporting service:

  • Establish when it pays to clean hulls and polish propellers
  • Evaluate how different hull treatments are affecting fuel efficiency
  • Compare the Total Ownership Cost of underwater coating systems
  • Assess the true parameters in slow steaming optimization
  • Implementing a CO2 index based on speed, displacement and propulsion condition

CHARTER NEGOTIATIONS

Many of our customers rely on our Actual Obtainable Speed/Consumption Diagrams as decision support in attaining highest charter rates based on known hull and propeller condition and defined weather allowances.

Our customers also use our analysis for slow steaming and super slow steaming metrics for precise speed and fuel oil consumption.

DRY DOCK ANALYSIS

Our services include review of husbandry reports (propeller polishing, hull cleaning) and docking reports.

These reports usually include photos of the hull and propeller, before and after, and are utilized by PDI to enhance our database of correlating added resistance (hull and propeller performance losses) to actual visual conditions.
